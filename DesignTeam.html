What Have I Done?

Plaquate

January 2016 I joined a team of 7 other undergraduate students working to design a safer coronary atherectomy device for the treatment of atheroslerosis . Our team worked with Johns Hopkins doctors and Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory engineers to design the device with both medical and mechanical concerns in mind.
Our team was awarded a stage 1 Venture Well Grant giving our team funding as well as the opportunity to attend specialized business plan and entrepreneurship workshops.
Our team won second place in the Johns Hopkins Business Plan competition for our device Plaquate. The device was designed to utilize a specific method of ablation to remove arterial plaque while not damaging arterial walls.
