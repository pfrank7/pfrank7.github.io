My Cheap Guide to Europe

Edinburgh

I am studying abroad in Edinburgh, Scotland for a semester. At first I was aiming to make a blog detailing all my ~adventures~ but I have instead opted for what I hope can be a somewhat valuable resource. A guide to traveling cheaply in Europe for the poor college student. Unfortunately, I'm only able to give detailed advice on the places I was able to sneak off to but I hope it can be of use to someone out there.

Edinburgh

Arthur's Seat

Jan 10, 2017

Arthur's seat is one of the highest points near Edinburgh. It is easily walkable from the city center. All you have to do is put the big hill in your sights and head in that direction until you reach it. There is a fairly obvious path that then leads you up the hill to the top. It will start out as paved but end up rockier as you start to climb. I'd give yourself 30 minutes to make it to the top. I went in January and while the walk warmed me up the top was incredibly cold and windy, the wind being something thats hard to escape no matter where you go in Edinburgh. This lovely little excursion is completely free of charge and one of the "must dos" when it come to the city of Edinburgh. I recommend hiking it near sunrise or sunset, sunset being the more dangerous of the two as you'll be hiking back down in the dark.

Paris

General Info

Jan 18, 2017

My first weekend getaway was to the City of Love. A last minute round trip flight cost around 115 US dollars but I probably could have found a cheaper one if I had planned a little farther in advance. When I say last minute too, I mean I purchased the ticket 2 hours before the flight was scheduled to take off. Not a bad price when you take that into consideration. In general Paris is not the cheapest European city to visit but it can be done. I traveled with 5 friends and we stayed in a hostel. Because there were so many of us, we were able to get an entire room to ourselves. The cheapest places to stay do tend to be hostels. There may be cheap Air B&B's available but they are most likely not as centrally located. From our hostel we were able to walk to almost everything we wanted to see.

The per night price for our hostel was not particularly cheap coming in at around 50 US dollars a night. This was primarily due to not booking ahead of time. So while it is possible to just show up in the city and find accommodations, if you want the best price you should take the time to plan ahead. The cheapest beds tend to be in dorm style rooms in hostels. Private rooms are available but they may be slightly pricier. Each has its pro's and con's. We were particularly lucky in that our hostel provided breakfast each morning, not all do. Overall you are going to want to be sure to read the reviews of wherever you choose to stay. Also know that if you are staying in a dorm bed you may not have anywhere to store valuables while you are exploring the city so either don't bring them, or plan to carry them with you.

Harry Potter?

Yet another free opportunity to take advantage of is the many free tours offered in Edinburgh including the Potter Tour we went on. The tour takes you around the city pointing out many of the inspirations and key places that led to the creation of the famous wizard series. For the dedicated Potter fan you can also take the time to grab a cup of coffee in the Elephant House, a cafe famous for the time J.K. Rowling spent there while writing her books. You can also take the time to check out the Balmoral Hotel where Rowling finished the last Harry Potter book. Tips and Tricks

I recommend budgeting yourself in pounds during your time in the Edinburgh. Its far to hard to convert back and forth between pounds and dollars every time you make a purchase and if you don't watch your spending at all its very easy to spend far more than you imagined. One pound converts to almost 1.4 US dollars.
